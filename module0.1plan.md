Module 0.1 Core Concept Track
Card 1 — Mission Brief: You Are Not Here for Tips

Card type: Mission brief
Visual: Strong academy/training-system header, not a generic callout
Purpose: Break the influencer-content mindset.

What this card must teach:
Most people enter markets by consuming random tips, videos, indicators, and screenshots. That creates activity, not skill. Lurnava trains the opposite: structured observation, controlled simulation, testing, and review.

Real situation to include:
A beginner watches three videos in one night. Each video says something different. The learner opens the chart with confidence, but cannot explain what they are looking for. That is not learning; that is borrowed conviction.

Failure prevented:
Random content consumption and false confidence.

Must not say:
“Become a professional trader”, “win”, “profit”, “secret strategy”.

Card 2 — The Lurnava Training Loop

Card type: Visual intro
Visual: Coded interactive loop
Steps: Learn → Practice → Test/Mission → Journal Review

What this card must teach:
Skill is not built by reading only. A learner must study a concept, apply it in simulation, prove recognition through a test or mission, and review the thinking afterward.

Analogy to use:
Flight school or driving school.
A pilot does not only read manuals. They study controls, train in a simulator, complete checklists, and review errors. Lurnava follows the same logic for market reading.

Failure prevented:
Skipping practice and review.

Learner interaction:
Click each step and see what it does.

Card 5 — One Hour of Bad Practice

Card type: Scenario
Visual: Scenario board: Bad Practice vs Deliberate Practice

Situation:
A learner has one hour at night. The chart is moving fast. They want to feel productive.

Bad practice path:
They jump between charts, click through practice actions, change ideas repeatedly, and finish with no notes. They were active for one hour but learned almost nothing.

Deliberate practice path:
They choose one concept, observe one situation, write one reason, mark one review condition, and save one journal note. They did less, but learned more.

Failure prevented:
Unstructured practice.

Core lesson:
More actions do not mean more learning. Better review creates better learning.

Card 6 — The Three-Question Gate

Card type: Rule / checkpoint
Visual: Gate checklist

Before moving forward, the learner must answer:

1. What did I observe?
2. What evidence supports it?
3. What would make me review this reading?

Pass example:
“I observed price moving inside a range. The evidence is that recent highs and lows are not breaking clearly. I will review the reading if price closes outside the range and holds.”

Fail example:
“I think it will move because it looks strong.”

Failure prevented:
Acting from unsupported assumptions.

Card 7 — Training Drill: The Hype Trap

Card type: Practice
Visual: Decision scenario or choice task

Scenario:
A learner watches a short online video claiming a candle pattern works well. They open the Lab and see something similar.

Question:
What should the learner do first?

Correct answer:
Identify the actual concept, check chart context, practice deliberately in simulation, and write a review note.

Plausible wrong answers:

Apply it immediately because the candle looks similar.
Change timeframes until the idea looks correct.
Skip the journal because this is only practice.
Watch more videos instead of testing the concept.

Failure prevented:
External hype controlling the learning process.

Card 8 — Debrief: What You Are Being Trained For

Card type: Summary / bridge
Visual: Final training identity card

What this card must say:
You are being trained to explain your reading, not memorize tips. You are being trained to observe, test, and review under uncertainty. The first goal is not confidence. The first goal is clarity.

Bridge to Module 0.2:
Next, the learner studies what a market actually is: not just a moving line, but an interaction between buyers, sellers, available orders, and price.

Module 0.1 Forex Adaptation Track

This track should not repeat the core lesson. It should show how the Lurnava training system applies to Forex.

Forex Card 1 — Forex Training Brief: You Are Reading a Relationship

Purpose:
Teach that Forex is not one asset moving alone.

Key point:
In Forex, every chart is a relationship between two currencies. EUR/USD is not just “EUR moving.” It is EUR compared against USD.

Visual:
Currency pair relationship diagram: Base Currency vs Quote Currency.

Real situation:
A learner sees EUR/USD rising and says, “EUR is strong.” But maybe USD is weakening faster. Without understanding the relationship, the learner misreads the chart.

Failure prevented:
Treating currency pairs like single assets.

Forex Card 2 — Forex Training Loop Example

Purpose:
Apply Learn → Practice → Test → Review to Forex.

Example:

Learn: base/quote relationship
Practice: inspect two currency pairs involving USD
Test/Mission: identify whether movement is base strength or quote weakness
Journal: write what made the reading clear or unclear

Visual:
Small workflow board for a Forex learner.

Forex Card 3 — Common Forex Beginner Failure

Scenario:
A learner sees GBP/USD moving fast during London session. They assume the pair is “strong” without checking session context, spread, or whether USD is moving broadly.

Lesson:
Forex requires relationship reading plus time awareness.

Failure prevented:
Reading Forex as isolated candle movement.

Forex Card 4 — Forex Mini Drill

Question:
EUR/USD rises while USD appears weak across several USD pairs. What is the better beginner reading?

Correct idea:
The rise may be partly explained by USD weakness, so the learner should treat broader USD context as supporting information, not as a standalone instruction.

Wrong ideas:

“EUR must always be strong.”
“The candle alone is enough.”
“Ignore the quote currency.”
Module 0.1 Gold Adaptation Track

Gold needs a different feel. It should introduce that Gold is not just “another chart.”

Gold Card 1 — Gold Training Brief: Speed Is Not Clarity

Purpose:
Teach that Gold often moves sharply, but sharp movement does not automatically mean clear reading.

Visual:
Clean movement vs sharp noisy movement comparison.

Real situation:
A learner sees a fast Gold candle and feels urgency. But urgency is not evidence. Gold can move sharply because of USD pressure, rates expectations, market uncertainty, or thin liquidity.

Failure prevented:
Confusing speed with clarity.

Gold Card 2 — Gold Training Loop Example

Example:

Learn: Gold context drivers
Practice: compare Gold movement with USD context
Test/Mission: identify whether the chart is clear or noisy
Journal: note whether the reading had enough evidence or was only reaction to speed

Visual:
Gold training loop with USD / rates / uncertainty as context labels.

Gold Card 3 — Common Gold Beginner Failure

Scenario:
A learner opens XAU/USD, sees a long wick, and immediately assumes reversal. But the wick appears during a fast macro-sensitive move. The better response is to review structure and context before forming a reading.

Lesson:
Gold requires extra review discipline.

Failure prevented:
Reacting to Gold volatility without context.

Gold Card 4 — Gold Mini Drill

Question:
Gold makes a large candle with long wicks in both directions during a volatile period. What is the better beginner response?

Correct idea:
Classify the condition as noisy or unclear until structure gives better evidence.

Wrong ideas:

“Big movement means strong clarity.”
“The wick alone confirms the next move.”
“Fast charts should be practiced faster.”
Module 0.1 Crypto Adaptation Track

Crypto must teach 24/7, fragmentation, and hype resistance immediately.

Crypto Card 1 — Crypto Training Brief: Always Open Does Not Mean Always Clear

Purpose:
Teach that Crypto trades 24/7, but not every period has useful learning quality.

Visual:
Forex sessions vs Crypto always-open timeline.

Real situation:
A learner opens BTC on a weekend, sees a sudden move, and assumes the market is giving a clean lesson. But weekend liquidity can be different, and exchange behavior can vary.

Failure prevented:
Treating all crypto movement as equally reliable.

Crypto Card 2 — Crypto Training Loop Example

Example:

Learn: spot/perp/funding basics
Practice: inspect whether movement is broad or exchange-specific
Test/Mission: classify the clue as spot, perp, funding-related, or unclear
Journal: record what made the move trustworthy or questionable

Visual:
Crypto loop with Spot / Perps / Funding / Liquidation Context.

Crypto Card 3 — Common Crypto Beginner Failure

Scenario:
A learner sees social media hype around a coin, opens a chart, and sees fast movement. They mistake public excitement for evidence. A Lurnava learner checks whether the chart, market context, and mechanics support the reading.

Lesson:
Crypto requires hype filtering.

Failure prevented:
Letting social reaction replace market reading.

Crypto Card 4 — Crypto Mini Drill

Question:
BTC moves sharply while funding is high and many learners online are excited. What is the better beginner response?

Correct idea:
Treat funding and public reaction as supporting context to review carefully, not as a direct instruction.

Wrong ideas:

“Everyone is excited, so it must continue.”
“Funding alone tells the answer.”
“Crypto moves fast, so review is optional.”
