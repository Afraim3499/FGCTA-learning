## 1. Core Curriculum

### 1.1 Visual Intro: The Road to Market Reading
**Label**: The Journey
**Visual Key**: `learning-loop-visual`
**Body**: Market reading is a skill developed through a cycle of observation, classification, and feedback. You are not learning to predict; you are learning to read objective records of market participant behavior.

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **Observation**: The act of identifying factual data on a chart without bias.
    *   **Classification**: Assigning a structural meaning to the observed data.
    *   **Feedback**: Reviewing the outcome to refine the reading process.
*   **Why This Matters**: Most beginners try to guess what happens next. Professionals focus on reading what has already happened to build a probability-based conclusion.
*   **Real Life Example**: Like learning to read music or a new language, you first learn the symbols (candles), then the grammar (structure), then the story (narrative).
*   **Common Mistake**: Trying to 'predict' the future instead of 'reading' the present.
*   **Quick Note**: Focus on the process, not the prediction.

### 1.2 Concept: The Evidence-First Mindset
**Label**: Core Philosophy
**Visual Key**: `evidence-logic-board`
**Body**: At Lurnava, we do not follow 'signals' or 'gurus.' We follow the evidence. If the chart does not provide clear structural facts, the professional answer is "I don't know yet."

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **Evidence**: Factual, observable chart behavior (e.g., a break of a high).
    *   **Signal**: A generic instruction to buy or sell (e.g., "Buy at this price").
    *   **Logic**: The reasoning that connects evidence to a conclusion.
*   **Why This Matters**: Signals fail when market conditions change. Evidence-based logic allows you to adapt to any market environment.
*   **Real Life Example**: A detective doesn't 'guess' who committed a crime; they follow the fingerprints and DNA. In the market, price is the DNA.
*   **Common Mistake**: Looking for a "magic indicator" that tells you what to do.
*   **Quick Note**: Data over drama. Logic over luck.

### 1.3 Concept: The Anatomy of a Reading
**Label**: Workflow
**Visual Key**: `reading-anatomy-visual`
**Body**: A professional reading consists of three layers: **Context** (The Big Picture), **Structure** (The Roadmap), and **Price Action** (The Immediate Detail). Alignment across these layers creates high-probability evidence.

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **Context**: The environment (e.g., Is the market in a major range?).
    *   **Structure**: The sequence of highs and lows (The 'skeleton' of the chart).
    *   **Price Action**: The behavior of individual candles at key levels.
*   **Why This Matters**: If you only look at one layer, you miss the full story. A 'buy' candle in a 'sell' context is usually a trap.
*   **Real Life Example**: In driving, Context is the weather; Structure is the road; Price Action is the movement of the car right in front of you.
*   **Common Mistake**: Thinking a single candle is enough for a full decision.
*   **Quick Note**: Big picture first, detail last.

### 1.4 Practice: Market Reading vs. Prediction
**Label**: Interactive Drill
**Visual Key**: `drill-prediction-vs-reading`
**Body**: This drill tests your ability to distinguish between a prediction (guessing) and a reading (observing).

#### Task:
*   **Question**: A learner sees a price reaching a previous high and says, "It's definitely going to crash here!" What is this?
*   **Options**:
    *   **A**: A professional reading based on history.
    *   **B**: A prediction based on assumption. (Correct)
    *   **C**: A high-probability logic.
    *   **D**: A factual observation.
*   **Feedback**:
    *   **A**: Incorrect. History suggests it *might* react, but saying it *definitely* will is a prediction, not a reading.
    *   **B**: Correct. Assuming an outcome before the market provides evidence (like a rejection or shift) is a prediction-based trap.
    *   **C**: Incorrect. Logic requires evidence. No evidence has been provided yet in this scenario.
    *   **D**: Incorrect. Factual observation would be "Price has reached a previous high," not a conclusion about what it will do.

### 1.5 Summary: Module Debrief
**Label**: Module Recap
**Visual Key**: `module-recap-map`
**Body**: You have successfully completed the first step of your journey. You now understand that Lurnava is about **Evidence over Prediction** and **Reading over Guessing**.

## 2. Forex Adaptation

### 2.1 Visual Intro: The Forex Roadway
**Label**: Market Context
**Visual Key**: `forex-roadway-visual`
**Body**: The Forex market is a global tug-of-war between currencies. You are not buying a stock; you are reading the relative strength of one economy against another.

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **Currency Pair**: The relationship between two currencies (e.g., EUR/USD).
    *   **Exchange Rate**: The value of one currency expressed in another.
    *   **Relative Strength**: How strong one currency is compared to another.
*   **Why This Matters**: In Forex, a move can be caused by the first currency getting stronger *or* the second currency getting weaker. You must read both sides of the story.
*   **Real Life Example**: A game of tug-of-war. If the center flag moves, it’s because one side is pulling harder or the other side is letting go.
*   **Common Mistake**: Thinking you are only trading 'one' thing.
*   **Quick Note**: Forex is a market of relationships.

### 2.2 Concept: Base and Quote Logic
**Label**: The Mechanics
**Visual Key**: `base-quote-visual`
**Body**: In a pair like EUR/USD, the first currency (EUR) is the **Base** and the second (USD) is the **Quote**. If the chart goes up, the Base is strengthening or the Quote is weakening.

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **Base Currency**: The 'unit' you are trading.
    *   **Quote Currency**: The 'money' used to measure the unit.
    *   **Pip**: The smallest unit of price movement in Forex.
*   **Why This Matters**: Understanding the 'unit' vs 'money' relationship prevents confusion when reading chart direction.
*   **Real Life Example**: Buying apples with dollars. The apple is the Base; the dollar is the Quote. If the 'apple chart' goes up, apples are more expensive.
*   **Common Mistake**: Confusing which currency is driving the move.
*   **Quick Note**: Base = The Item. Quote = The Price.

### 2.3 Practice: Identifying the Driver
**Label**: Practical Drill
**Visual Key**: `forex-driver-drill`
**Body**: EUR/USD is moving up sharply. At the same time, the US Dollar Index (DXY) is crashing. What is the most likely driver of the EUR/USD move?

#### Task:
*   **Question**: What is the most likely driver?
*   **Options**:
    *   **A**: Euro Strength
    *   **B**: US Dollar Weakness (Correct)
    *   **C**: Both are equally strong
    *   **D**: The chart is broken
*   **Feedback**:
    *   **A**: Possible, but since the DXY (Dollar Index) is crashing, the most direct evidence points to the Dollar.
    *   **B**: Correct. If the Dollar is weakening globally (DXY), it will cause EUR/USD to rise even if the Euro itself is neutral.
    *   **C**: Incorrect. If both were strong, the pair would move sideways.
    *   **D**: Incorrect. This is a standard market relationship.

## 3. Gold Adaptation

### 3.1 Visual Intro: The Gold Roadway
**Label**: Market Context
**Visual Key**: `gold-roadway-visual`
**Body**: Gold (XAU/USD) is a unique asset. It is both a commodity and a global currency. It often moves based on "fear" and "safety" rather than just economic growth.

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **XAU/USD**: The symbol for Gold priced in US Dollars.
    *   **Safe Haven**: An asset people buy when they are afraid of the economy.
    *   **Yield**: Gold does not pay interest, making it sensitive to interest rates.
*   **Why This Matters**: Gold doesn't behave like a currency pair. It reacts sharply to geopolitical stress and inflation.
*   **Real Life Example**: Gold is like 'financial insurance.' People buy it when they think the 'house' (the global economy) might be in trouble.
*   **Common Mistake**: Treating Gold like a regular Forex pair.
*   **Quick Note**: Gold is the ultimate record of global uncertainty.

### 3.2 Concept: Gold vs. The US Dollar
**Label**: The Inverse Link
**Visual Key**: `gold-usd-visual`
**Body**: Gold is priced in Dollars. This means if the Dollar gets stronger, Gold usually gets cheaper (all else being equal). This inverse relationship is a core part of the Gold roadway.

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **Inverse Correlation**: When one thing goes up, the other usually goes down.
    *   **USD Strength**: The global value of the US Dollar.
    *   **Purchasing Power**: How much Gold a single Dollar can buy.
*   **Why This Matters**: You cannot read Gold without reading the Dollar. They are two sides of the same scale.
*   **Real Life Example**: A seesaw. If the Dollar side goes up, the Gold side usually goes down.
*   **Common Mistake**: Buying Gold while the US Dollar is in a massive uptrend.
*   **Quick Note**: Watch the Dollar to understand the Gold.

### 3.3 Practice: The Safe Haven Shift
**Label**: Practical Drill
**Visual Key**: `gold-safe-haven-drill`
**Body**: Geopolitical tension suddenly increases. Global stock markets are falling. How does Gold typically react in this environment?

#### Task:
*   **Question**: How does Gold typically react?
*   **Options**:
    *   **A**: It falls because people need cash.
    *   **B**: It rises as investors seek 'safety.' (Correct)
    *   **C**: It stays exactly the same.
    *   **D**: It follows the stock market down.
*   **Feedback**:
    *   **A**: Rare, but only in extreme 'liquidity' crises. Usually, it's the opposite.
    *   **B**: Correct. Gold's historical role is a 'store of value' during times of high uncertainty and market stress.
    *   **C**: Incorrect. Gold is highly sensitive to global events.
    *   **D**: Incorrect. Gold often has an 'inverse' relationship with stocks during crises.

## 4. Crypto Adaptation

### 4.1 Visual Intro: The Crypto Roadway
**Label**: Market Context
**Visual Key**: `crypto-roadway-visual`
**Body**: Crypto is the most volatile roadway. It is driven by technology, adoption, and extreme speculation. Unlike Gold or Forex, it is a 24/7 market that never sleeps.

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **Volatility**: The speed and size of price changes.
    *   **24/7 Market**: Trading happens every second of every day.
    *   **Decentralization**: No central bank or government controls the market.
*   **Why This Matters**: Crypto moves faster and further than any other market. This requires much higher discipline to avoid emotional 'FOMO.'
*   **Real Life Example**: Crypto is like a 'digital frontier.' It's fast, exciting, and full of opportunity, but also lacks the guardrails of older markets.
*   **Common Mistake**: Entering Crypto for 'fast money' without a process.
*   **Quick Note**: Crypto rewards discipline and punishes emotion.

### 4.2 Concept: Bitcoin as the North Star
**Label**: Market Correlation
**Visual Key**: `btc-dominance-visual`
**Body**: Most of the Crypto market follows Bitcoin. If Bitcoin moves, the 'Altcoins' (smaller cryptos) usually follow, but with even more volatility.

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **Bitcoin (BTC)**: The first and largest cryptocurrency.
    *   **Altcoins**: Any cryptocurrency other than Bitcoin.
    *   **Correlation**: How closely two assets move together.
*   **Why This Matters**: You cannot read the 'small' cryptos without first checking the 'Big' one. Bitcoin sets the environment.
*   **Real Life Example**: Bitcoin is the 'Sun' of the crypto solar system. When it flares up, all the planets feel the heat.
*   **Common Mistake**: Trying to trade Altcoins while Bitcoin is crashing.
*   **Quick Note**: BTC leads, the market follows.

### 4.3 Practice: Filtering the Noise
**Label**: Practical Drill
**Visual Key**: `crypto-noise-drill`
**Body**: A small crypto token is up 50% in one hour on a random news tweet. Bitcoin is down 5% and in a downtrend. What is the disciplined crypto reading?

#### Task:
*   **Question**: What is the disciplined reading?
*   **Options**:
    *   **A**: Buy immediately; it's going to the moon!
    *   **B**: Ignore the move; it's likely 'noise' and lacks broad market support. (Correct)
    *   **C**: Sell Bitcoin and buy this token.
    *   **D**: Technicals don't matter in crypto.
*   **Feedback**:
    *   **A**: Incorrect. This is 'FOMO' and usually leads to buying the top of a temporary spike.
    *   **B**: Correct. A single token moving against a weak broad market (BTC down) is high-risk and often unsustainable 'noise.'
    *   **C**: Incorrect. This is emotional 'chasing.'
    *   **D**: Incorrect. Technicals and correlation matter *more* in volatile markets.
