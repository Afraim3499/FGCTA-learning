# Module 0.3: Candles Are Records, Not Signals

**Objective**: Learn to read price data as objective evidence of participant interaction.

---

## 1. Core Curriculum

### 1.1 Visual Intro: Candle Anatomy
**Label**: Visual First
**Visual Key**: `candle-anatomy`
**Body**: A candle is a time-boxed record. It shows where price opened, how high and low it travelled, and where it closed during that period. It is evidence of participant commitment, not a direct instruction to act.

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **Open/Close**: The prices at the start and end of the period.
    *   **High/Low**: The extremes reached during the period.
    *   **Body**: The area between Open and Close; shows the net result.
    *   **Wick**: The lines showing prices that were tested but rejected.
*   **Why This Matters**: Every part of the candle answers a question. The body shows who 'won' the period. The wicks show where one side tried to push but failed. Without this anatomy, you are just looking at colors.
*   **Real Life Example**: A 5-minute candle on EUR/USD. If it opens at 1.1000, moves to 1.1012, drops to 1.0998, and closes at 1.1009, that candle records the full journey of that period.
*   **Common Mistake**: Thinking one candle is a prediction. Beginners see a green candle and instantly think 'buy.'
*   **Quick Note**: A candle records a period of market activity.

### 1.2 Concept: Candles Are Records, Not Signals
**Label**: Core Principle
**Visual Key**: `record-vs-signal`
**Body**: A candle does not give an instruction. It records movement. A Lurnava learner does not ask, “What does this candle tell me to do?” They ask, “What market behavior did this candle record?”

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **Record**: An objective history of price action during a set window.
    *   **Signal Trap**: Treating a candle shape as an automatic buy/sell instruction.
    *   **Market Behavior**: The collective interaction of buyers and sellers.
*   **Why This Matters**: If you treat candles as magic signals, you will lose discipline. If you treat them as records, you gain clarity.
*   **Real Life Example**: A fast move after a news release. The candle is recording aggressive buying or selling during that moment. It is evidence of behavior, not magic.
*   **Common Mistake**: Treating candles like signal stickers. Beginners often label a candle 'bullish' and stop thinking.
*   **Quick Note**: The candle is the record. The reading comes from context.

### 1.3 Concept: Body, Wick, and Close Location
**Label**: Candle Mechanics
**Visual Key**: `candle-evidence-board`
**Body**: The body records net commitment. Wicks record rejected pressure. The close location shows where the balance of power shifted at the final moment of the record.

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **Rejection**: When price reaches a level but cannot be maintained.
    *   **Commitment**: When price holds a direction and closes there.
    *   **Balance of Power**: The net result of buyer vs. seller interaction at the close.
*   **Why This Matters**: Color is a shortcut. Anatomy is the full story.
*   **Real Life Example**: The 'Flash Rejection'. In high-speed markets like Gold, a spike up followed by a crash leaves a long upper wick—a record of that failed attempt.
*   **Common Mistake**: Ignoring the close location. A candle can be green but close near its low, meaning sellers took control at the end.
*   **Quick Note**: Color is not enough. Read the whole record.

### 1.4 Concept: Context Changes the Reading
**Label**: Context Logic
**Visual Key**: `context-logic-board`
**Body**: The same candle shape can mean different things depending on where it forms. A long wick in the middle of a range is just noise; the same wick at a major resistance level is significant evidence.

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **Location**: Where a candle forms relative to previous price structure.
    *   **Structure**: The 'landscape' of the market (ranges, trends, levels).
    *   **Contextual Weight**: Importance given to a record based on its surroundings.
*   **Why This Matters**: Pattern memorization is dangerous without location. Context is the judge.
*   **Real Life Example**: The 'Resistance Wick'. A rejection wick at a major historical high has much more weight than a random wick in a messy range.
*   **Common Mistake**: Reading candles in a vacuum. Never read the record without checking the location.
*   **Quick Note**: Same shape. Different context. Different reading.

### 1.5 Practice: Practice Check: Candle or Signal?
**Label**: Interactive Task
**Visual Key**: `candle-decision-board`
**Body**: A candle is evidence, not a full decision. The disciplined learner reads what the candle recorded, checks where it formed, and waits for context before forming a stronger reading.

#### Task:
*   **Question**: A learner sees a candle with a long lower wick and immediately buys, saying 'It's a reversal signal!' What is the disciplined response?
*   **Options**:
    *   **A**: The wick guarantees reversal.
    *   **B**: The candle is useful evidence, but it must be checked with location, close, structure, and context. (Correct)
    *   **C**: The candle color is the only thing that matters.
    *   **D**: Candle patterns should be followed without review.
*   **Feedback**:
    *   **A**: Incorrect. A wick suggests rejection but does not guarantee reversal.
    *   **B**: Correct. A candle records what happened, but meaning depends on context. The wick is evidence, not a complete conclusion.
    *   **C**: Incorrect. Color is only one small part of the record.
    *   **D**: Incorrect. Pattern-following without review creates weak reasoning.

### 1.6 Summary: Module Debrief: Candles Are Records
**Label**: Module Debrief
**Visual Key**: `candle-recap-map`
**Body**: You have successfully moved from 'Shape Recognition' to 'Record Reading.' You now understand that a candle is a visual summary of participant interaction.

## 2. Forex Adaptation

### 2.1 Visual Intro: Forex Candles Are Quote Records
**Label**: Forex Candle Foundation
**Visual Key**: `forex-quote-record`
**Body**: A Forex candle is a time-boxed quote record. It shows where the quoted price opened, how high and low it travelled, and where it closed during that selected period. It is evidence from a price feed, not a universal signal.

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **Quote Feed**: The stream of price data provided by a broker.
    *   **Pip**: The standard unit of price change in Forex.
    *   **Timeframe**: The duration of activity recorded by a single candle.
*   **Why This Matters**: A Forex candle is a record of quoted movement in a specific pair and platform environment. This context matters before interpretation.
*   **Real Life Example**: A 15-minute EUR/USD candle records the quote movement in pips during that 15-minute window.
*   **Common Mistake**: Thinking every Forex candle is a complete picture of the whole global market.
*   **Quick Note**: A Forex candle is a quote record first.

### 2.2 Visual Intro: Pip Range, Body, and Wick
**Label**: Forex Candle Mechanics
**Visual Key**: `forex-pip-candle-board`
**Body**: In Forex, candle size should be read in pips and context. A large-looking candle on one pair may be normal, while the same pip movement on another pair may be unusual. Read the body, wick, close, and range together.

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **Pip Range**: Total distance from High to Low in pips.
    *   **Pair Behavior**: Typical movement characteristics of a specific pair.
    *   **Volatility**: The degree of variation in trading price over time.
*   **Why This Matters**: judge candles by appearance only is a mistake. Size must be understood in pips, timeframe, and pair behavior.
*   **Real Life Example**: 15 pips on EUR/USD in a quiet period stands out; the same move in an active session may be ordinary.
*   **Common Mistake**: Comparing candle size visually without checking pip count or timeframe.
*   **Quick Note**: In Forex, measure the candle before judging it.

### 2.3 Visual Intro: Spread and Session Can Distort Candles
**Label**: Quote Quality
**Visual Key**: `forex-distorted-candle-board`
**Body**: A Forex candle should not be read without quote condition. During thin liquidity, rollover, or unstable spread, a wick may reflect poor quote quality as much as clean market intent.

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **Spread**: Difference between bid and ask price.
    *   **Rollover**: Period of day-to-day transition with often thin liquidity.
    *   **Distorted Candle**: A record impacted by technical noise rather than market intent.
*   **Why This Matters**: Some candles look dramatic because market condition is weak. Ignoring spread and session context may lead to mistaking noise for evidence.
*   **Real Life Example**: A long wick near rollover with wide spread—rushed learners call it rejection; trained learners check quote quality.
*   **Common Mistake**: Treating every Forex wick as meaningful without checking spread and session.
*   **Quick Note**: A candle is only as useful as the condition it formed in.

## 3. Crypto Adaptation

### 3.1 Visual Intro: Crypto Candles Are Venue Records
**Label**: Venue Fragmentation
**Visual Key**: `crypto-candle-source-map`
**Body**: In Crypto, price is fragmented across dozens of venues. A move can look like a clean trend on one exchange and a distorted spike on another. Never trust a candle until you know which venue produced it.

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **Spot/Perpetual/DEX**: Different venue types with different pricing drivers.
    *   **Aggregated Feed**: A price view built from multiple sources.
    *   **Index/Mark Price**: Reference prices used for risk management.
*   **Why This Matters**: A crypto candle may not represent the whole market. Reading one without knowing its source may lead to mistaking venue noise for broad evidence.
*   **Real Life Example**: BTC spikes on a perpetual exchange while spot markets move less—trained learners ask if it's spot-supported or derivatives-driven.
*   **Common Mistake**: Thinking one crypto candle equals the whole market.
*   **Quick Note**: In crypto, first ask: where did this candle come from?

### 3.2 Visual Intro: The Two Market Engines
**Label**: Spot vs Perpetual
**Visual Key**: `two-candle-engines-board`
**Body**: Crypto has two primary engines: **Spot** (Asset trades) and **Perpetual** (Contract/Leverage pressure). Prioritize the Spot Engine as the 'True' record and treat Perp-only moves as potential noise.

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **Spot Market**: Actual asset buying and selling.
    *   **Perpetual Market**: Derivatives based on leverage and funding pressure.
    *   **Liquidation Risk**: Forced closing of leveraged positions.
*   **Why This Matters**: Spot reflects actual asset trading. Perpetual can reflect leverage and forced exits. Reading them the same way misses the source of the pressure.
*   **Real Life Example**: Large green candle on BTC perpetual while spot movement is smaller—check if spot supports the move.
*   **Common Mistake**: Reading spot and perpetual candles as carrying the same weight.
*   **Quick Note**: Same shape. Different market engine. Different reading.

### 3.3 Visual Intro: Diagnosing Mechanical Wicks
**Label**: Wick Diagnosis
**Visual Key**: `wick-source-diagnostic-board`
**Body**: Crypto wicks are often 'mechanical anomalies' like liquidations or thin liquidity spikes. Before assuming 'rejection,' diagnose its source. No spot volume usually means the wick is mechanical noise.

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **Liquidation Wick**: Shaped by forced closing of leveraged positions.
    *   **Thin Liquidity**: When few orders allow price to move quickly.
    *   **Mechanical Movement**: Movement shaped by market mechanics, not intent.
*   **Why This Matters**: Dramatic wicks don't always mean clean rejection. Diagnose what created the wick before trusting it as evidence.
*   **Real Life Example**: Token drops, leaves long wick, and recovers—was it spot demand or just a liquidation event?
*   **Common Mistake**: Calling every long crypto wick “rejection” without checking mechanics.
*   **Quick Note**: In crypto, diagnose the wick before reading the candle.

## 4. Gold Adaptation

### 4.1 Visual Intro: Gold Candles Are Volatility Records
**Label**: Gold Candle Foundation
**Visual Key**: `gold-candle-context-board`
**Body**: Gold candles record XAU/USD movement, but their quality depends heavily on session activity, USD context, news pressure, and liquidity. Read the candle and the environment together.

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **Volatility**: Speed and sharpness of price movement.
    *   **USD Context**: Broad strength/weakness of the Dollar affecting Gold.
    *   **News Window**: Period around economic events where candles are unstable.
*   **Why This Matters**: Speed alone does not make a candle reliable. Know whether it formed in a clean environment or during unstable volatility.
*   **Real Life Example**: Large candle in NY session after data—check USD context and liquidity before trusting the size.
*   **Common Mistake**: Judging a Gold candle only by size or color.
*   **Quick Note**: In Gold, candle quality depends on context.

### 4.2 Visual Intro: News Candle vs. Structured Candle
**Label**: Candle Quality
**Visual Key**: `gold-candle-quality-board`
**Body**: A news candle may record unstable repricing. A structured candle is easier to read when the close, level, and follow-through support the move.

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **News Candle**: Formed during or near major economic events.
    *   **Repricing**: Fast price adjustment to new information.
    *   **Follow-Through**: Price behavior after the candle closes.
*   **Why This Matters**: News candles can trap learners with large moves that lack structure. Wait for structure before forming a strong reading.
*   **Real Life Example**: Gold spikes on news, leaves a wick, closes mid-range—wait for next candles to confirm structure.
*   **Common Mistake**: Thinking a large Gold news candle automatically gives a clear reading.
*   **Quick Note**: A dramatic candle is not always a clean candle.

### 4.3 Visual Intro: Gold Wick Diagnosis
**Label**: Wick Mechanics
**Visual Key**: `gold-wick-diagnostic-board`
**Body**: A Gold wick can be a rejection, but also a liquidity sweep or news spike. Diagnose the wick before trusting it as evidence.

#### Context Details:
*   **Key Terms**:
    *   **Liquidity Sweep**: Move beyond a level to trigger orders before returning.
    *   **Wick Diagnosis**: Identifying what may have created the wick.
    *   **Follow-Through**: Verification of the wick's intent.
*   **Why This Matters**: Calling every wick rejection leads to misreads. Ask what created it and if next behavior supports the reading.
*   **Real Life Example**: Gold pushes above a high, leaves upper wick—rejection, sweep, or news volatility?
*   **Common Mistake**: Treating every long Gold wick as a confirmed reversal.
*   **Quick Note**: A Gold wick is a clue, not a verdict.
