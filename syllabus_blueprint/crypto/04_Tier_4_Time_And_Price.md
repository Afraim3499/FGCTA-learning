# Tier 4: Time & Price (The 24/7 Cycle)

## Overview
Crypto never sleeps. Traditional Killzones don't apply the same way. We focus on the Asian Pump, weekend illiquidity, and the 8-hour funding rate cycle.

---

## Section 12: The Asian Pump
### Module 12.1: Algorithmic Buying in Tokyo Hours
- **Content**: Historically, Western sessions (NY) take profits, while Asian sessions (Tokyo/Seoul) aggressively bid up crypto assets. Identifying the 8:00 PM EST to 2:00 AM EST volume profile.

---

## Section 13: Weekend Illiquidity & CME Gaps
### Module 13.1: The CME Close
- **Content**: CME Bitcoin Futures close on Friday at 5:00 PM EST. The gap created between the Friday close and the Sunday open acts as a massive algorithmic magnet for the upcoming week.

### Module 13.2: Fake Weekend Breakouts
- **Content**: Why low-volume weekend breakouts are almost always traps set by market makers to engineer liquidity for the Monday NY open.

---

## Section 14: Funding Rate Settlement Windows
### Module 14.1: The 8-Hour Cycle
- **Content**: Perpetual contracts settle funding every 8 hours. Algorithms often spoof the orderbook leading into these settlements to game the funding payouts.

---

## Tier 4 Final Gate: The CME Gap Simulator
- **Format**: User is given a Friday close and a Sunday open. They must chart the CME gap, wait out the fake weekend breakout, and execute the gap-fill trade on Monday morning.
