# Tier 6: The Macro Matrix (Fiat & Central Banking)

## Overview
Forex is the largest market in the world. It cannot be manipulated by retail traders or even hedge funds. It is driven purely by Sovereign Wealth Funds, Commercial Banks, and Central Bank policy. This tier teaches you to read the macroeconomic data that dictates Interbank Price Delivery Algorithms (IPDA).

---

## Section 21: The Global Reserve (DXY)
*Goal: Understanding the US Dollar Index as the compass for all fiat pairs.*

### Module 21.1: The DXY Composition
- **Content**: The DXY is a geometric mean of 6 currencies, but heavily weighted toward the Euro (57.6%).
- **Rule**: If DXY is bullish, we do not look for longs on EUR/USD, GBP/USD, or AUD/USD. We only look for shorts.

### Module 21.2: SMT Divergence (Smart Money Tool)
- **Content**: Algorithms leave footprints when hedging. If DXY makes a Lower Low, but EUR/USD fails to make a Higher High, this divergence indicates massive institutional accumulation in the opposite direction.
- **Interactive Task**: `fiat_smt_scanner`
  - **Mechanic**: User analyzes 3 correlated charts (DXY, EUR/USD, GBP/USD). They must spot the exact hour where correlation breaks down (SMT Divergence) and execute a trade in the direction of the underlying strength.

## Section 22: Central Bank Policy (The True Drivers)
*Goal: Translating economic data into algorithmic draw on liquidity.*

### Module 22.1: Interest Rates & Carry Trade
- **Content**: Capital flows to the currency with the highest yield. If the FED raises rates while the ECB holds them flat, capital instantly drains from EUR into USD.
- **Concept**: The Carry Trade (earning swap interest by holding a high-yielding currency against a low-yielding one).

### Module 22.2: Forward Guidance & Speeches
- **Content**: Why markets react violently to the *words* "transitory" or "accommodative" during a Jerome Powell speech, even if the interest rate itself doesn't change.

## Section 23: The News Matrix (High Impact Events)
*Goal: Surviving NFP, CPI, and FOMC.*

### Module 23.1: The "Engineered" Liquidity Sweep
- **Content**: High-impact news releases do not "cause" price movement; they are used as an excuse by IPDA to rapidly inject volatility and sweep liquidity.
- **Rule**: Never trade the first 5 minutes of NFP. Wait for the initial algorithm to sweep retail stops (the Judas Swing), then trade the real macro expansion.

---

## Section 24: Commitment of Traders (COT) Data
*Goal: Tracking the Smart Money.*

### Module 24.1: Commercial vs Non-Commercial Hedging
- **Content**: The COT report is released every Friday. We track the "Commercials" (the actual banks and producers hedging risk) versus the "Non-Commercials" (large speculators).
- **Concept**: Commercials are always scaling into positions *against* the trend to hedge. When their net positions hit extreme historical limits, a massive macro reversal is imminent.

- **Format**: User is shown DXY, the COT report for EUR, and an upcoming CPI release calendar.
- **Requirement**: They must synthesize the COT data to determine the Commercial bias, identify the DXY SMT Divergence, and place a limit order *below* the current price to catch the engineered CPI liquidity sweep.
