# Tier 1: The Awakening (Fundamentals & Cognitive Shifts)

## Overview
This tier contains 4 comprehensive sections comprising 15 deep-dive modules. Before a student ever looks at a trading chart, we must destroy retail conditioning. We rebuild their psychology to think purely in probabilities, value exchange, and risk defense.

---

## Section 1: The Illusion of the Market
*Goal: Deconstruct the myth that trading is about "predicting the future."*

### Module 1.1: The True Purpose of Financial Markets
- **Content**: A historical deep-dive into why markets exist (facilitating trade, liquidity provision, hedging). 
- **Concept**: The market does not care about your indicators; it cares about finding fair value and fulfilling orders.

### Module 1.2: The "Zero-Sum" Fallacy vs. The Liquidity Transfer
- **Content**: Explaining that for every buyer, there is a seller. Institutional players cannot buy without immense sell pressure (liquidity).
- **Core Lesson**: You are not playing against a chart; you are playing against algorithms seeking liquidity.

### Module 1.3: The Probability Matrix (The Casino Analogy)
- **Content**: The casino doesn't know if the next spin is red or black, and they don't care. They have an edge.
- **Interactive Task**: `coin_flip_simulator`
  - **Mechanic**: User flips a weighted coin (55% win rate). They start with $1000. They must place 50 bets. They learn that even with an edge, they can experience 5 losses in a row.
  - **Pass Condition**: Complete the 50 flips without blowing the account by risking more than 5% per flip.

## Section 2: Rags to Riches (The Economics Simulator)
*Goal: Teach fundamental macro-economics (inflation, supply shocks, central banking) via an engaging gamified narrative.*

### Module 2.1: Supply, Demand, and Scarcity
- **Content**: The fundamental truth of value. If something is rare and desired, price rises.

### Module 2.2: Inflation and Fiat Currency (The Silent Thief)
- **Content**: Why $100 today buys less than $100 ten years ago. Introduction to Central Banking basics.

### Module 2.3: Interactive Mechanics (Rags to Riches Gameplay)
- **Task**: `macro_rags_to_riches`
  - **Level 1 (The Hustler)**: User collects scrap metal. A global supply chain shortage occurs (News Event). User must decide to hold inventory for higher prices or sell early.
  - **Level 2 (The Importer)**: User buys goods internationally. The Central Bank raises interest rates, making the local currency stronger. User must calculate if importing is now cheaper or more expensive.
  - **Level 3 (The Strategist)**: A war breaks out in an oil-producing nation. User must allocate capital between cash, oil, and gold.
  - **Pass Condition**: Achieve a net worth of $50,000 in the simulator.

---

## Section 3: The Psychology of the Void
*Goal: Confronting human cognitive biases that destroy traders.*

### Module 3.1: FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out) & The Pain of Action
- **Content**: The neurological dopamine loop of watching a green candle fly without you.

### Module 3.2: Revenge Trading & The Ego Trap
- **Content**: Why a trader immediately re-enters a losing trade with double the lot size to "make it back."

### Module 3.3: Loss Aversion
- **Content**: The psychological fact that losing $100 hurts twice as much as making $100 feels good. Why traders cut winners early and let losers run.

### Module 3.4: Interactive Task: The Tilt Simulator
- **Task**: `psychology_tilt_test`
  - **Mechanic**: A rapid-fire visual game where the user must click "Buy" only when a specific, rare pattern appears.
  - **The Twist**: The game intentionally feeds the user 10 false setups in a row, accompanied by ticking clocks and aggressive red flashes to induce stress.
  - **Pass Condition**: The user must refrain from clicking until the actual setup appears. Clicking out of frustration fails the module.

---

## Section 4: The Nature of Risk (Expected Value)
*Goal: Mathematical proof that risk management is the only holy grail.*

### Module 4.1: R-Multiples (Risk vs Reward)
- **Content**: Stop thinking in dollars. Start thinking in "R". If you risk $10 to make $30, that is a 3R trade.

### Module 4.2: Win Rate vs. Risk/Reward (The Matrix)
- **Content**: A trader with a 35% win rate who averages 1:3 RR is highly profitable. A trader with a 90% win rate who risks $10 to make $1 will eventually blow their account.

### Module 4.3: The Asymmetry of Drawdown
- **Content**: If you lose 50% of your account, you need a 100% gain just to get back to breakeven.
- **Interactive Task**: `drawdown_recovery_calculator`
  - **Mechanic**: User is presented with account balances that have suffered 10%, 20%, 50%, and 80% drawdowns. They must calculate the exact percentage gain required to recover.

- **Task**: `comprehensive_exam`
- **Format**: 20 rigorous questions covering Expected Value math, probability theory, supply/demand macro events, and cognitive bias identification.
- **Requirement**: 100% passing score required to unlock Tier 2 (Market Mechanics). Retries are allowed, but the question pool is randomized from a set of 100.
